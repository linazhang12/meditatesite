What is Meditation?

Meditation is an activity that focuses on bringing mindfulness, clarity, and calmness into daily life. While meditating, the mind is alert yet calm.

How do I meditate?

You can start meditated by positioning yourself in a comfortable position. You can sit anywhere or lay down on a bed. Start out by taking a few deep breaths, then focus on your breaths while allowing other thoughts to fade away in the background. Don't be worried if you have trouble concentrating at first - you'll get better with more practice.

Benefits of meditation

Meditation can reduce stress and stress-related conditions. In addition, it reduces anxiety, pain, and blood pressure, and it can help you sleep better. It will also allow you to develop more self-awareness and emotional-awareness.

History

The first recorded instances of meditation came from Asia. Indian scriptures focused on meditation, and Buddha, one of meditation's most iconic figures, encouraged meditation as a way of life. It traveled to the West in the mid-1900s and began to gain popularity. Although it is incorporated into religious practices, meditation can also be practiced without religion.

How to Incorporate It Into Your Life

It is easy to incorporate it into a busy life. Meditation can last anywhere from 5 minutes to 5 hours, but for busy people, you can meditate on a bus ride, while walking, or right before bed. It is also a good idea to meditate in the morning.

Resources
